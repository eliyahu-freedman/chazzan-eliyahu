About

Eliyahu Freedman is a High Holiday prayer leader (chazzan) from Toronto, Canada, raised in a family of chazzanim and ba'alei kriyah. He began his training with his cousin Yossi Sherman, developing an approach to tefillah that is both musically engaging and deeply participatory, helping communities connect to the emotional core of the Yamim Noraim.

He has further shaped his liturgical voice through study with Rabbi Elie Kaunfer, Rav Avi Weiss, and Reb Zalman Schachter-Shalomi, integrating traditional nusach with contemporary, heart-opening melodies.

For the past six years Eliyahu has been based in Israel, where he davened at Zichron Baruch — a Jaffa congregation whose services are led by beloved Israeli musician Akiva. That immersion in Israeli sacred music and communal prayer continues to shape his approach to leading.

Eliyahu has led High Holiday services seven times. His style weaves together classic Ashkenazic Yamim Noraim nusach with melodies from Carlebach, Joey Weisenberg, and Israeli folk traditions—creating services that are both rooted in tradition and accessible, uplifting and emotionally resonant.

Eliyahu is also a journalist who reported on the Israel–Gaza war for the Jewish Telegraphic Agency in the aftermath of October 7. Prior to working in journalism, he spent several years studying for rabbinic ordination at Yeshivat Chovevei Torah, completed a unit of Pastoral Education, and completed a Master's Degree in Talmudic Studies at Tel Aviv University.

Eliyahu is also available to offer accessible Torah learning and sermons on High Holiday themes, Jewish faith, memory, and the spiritual challenges of this difficult moment in Jewish life.

"The whole purpose of prayer is to crack open our hearts, our hardened hearts, just a little bit — and music is perhaps the best tool I've ever seen — I've ever experienced — for opening up a heart."

— Joey Weisenberg
